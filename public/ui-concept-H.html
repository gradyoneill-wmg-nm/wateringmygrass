01  —  the story

a rat who waters things and sees what grows

Watering My Grass is a live album recorded in April 2026 — a quiet document of a season spent close to the ground. Patient. Attentive. Watching things come back.

There are no grand gestures here. Just a hose. Some morning light. The slow science of growth. And music that sounds like what it feels like to tend something you care about.

02  —  the science

roots go down before anything comes up

Grass responds to deep, infrequent watering. Shallow daily watering keeps roots near the surface — fragile, dependent. But water deep, and the roots follow. They go down to find it.

There is a metaphor here that Rat did not labor over. It is simply true. The music was made the same way.

Recorded once. No overdubs. Whatever grew, grew on its own.
